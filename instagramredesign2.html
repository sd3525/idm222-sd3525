Instagram Algorithm Curator Redesign:
Overview:
Instagram is an app where users not only share and view photos and videos, but also explore their interests and view content that keeps them entertained.
Problem/Solution:
I found the content on my explore page to be irrelevant to my interests, so I conductected a survey to test if others felt the same and 8 out of 10 users agreed. In order to resolve this issue, I added an algorithm curator to the explore page where the user can control the accounts that are recommended to them.
Process:
Step #1: User Persona
According to Pew Research Center, the majority of Instagram’s users are between the age 18- to 29 (this is 71% of a nationally representative survey of 1,502 U.S. adults conducted via telephone from Jan. 25-Feb.8, 2021). I created a user persona to be able to better understand the average Instagram user:
Step #2: User Flow
I started off by creating a user flow with screenshots of the original app and adding my own algorithm creator screens to understand how a user would use the app with my new algorithm curator feature and where I could place new micro-interactions. I got my inspiration from Spotify’s Discover Weekly feature where a user can control what music suggestions they would receive on a weekly basis by liking or disliking songs.
Step #3: Branding
In order to not run into copyright issues with Instagram, I decided to change the name to Pixr as I felt that it still conveyed the main purpose of the app being centered around photos. For the logo I put 3 pixels that are red, green, and blue and made the accent colors these to reflect the basic rgb color model used in digital images. I chose the font saira and poppins because they are neutral, legible, and have a rectangular form to them which fits the pixel theme.
Step #4: Micro-interactions
I then brainstormed micro-interactions that I could include within my design and sketched out 20 possible ideas. I decided on a trash can for the posts that users dislike and a heart for posts that users like. I also wanted to include a feature where a user could chose to either message or follow the user without going into the accounts profile to streamline the process of finding the content that fits a user’s interests best. I created 10 iterations of this idea. These were my top four options:
Step #5: Low Fidelity Wireframes
I created low fidelity wireframes that I incorporated my final micro-interaction choices and placement of my algorithm curator in order to organize my content. I ended up deciding on the post turning into a small picture and falling into a heart if a user tapped it and the post crumpling up into a ball and falling into the trash can if a user tapped the trash. These give clear and quick visual cues as to whether a user likes or dislikes the content in a satisfying way. For the message and follow option I put different sized pixels in place where users could choose to either message or follow the user without going into the accounts profile to streamline the process of finding the content that fits a user’s interests best. I chose the Pixels in order to stay consistent with the logo and name of the app.

Step #8: Animating the Micro-Interactions in After Effects
I animated how the average user would interact with the app through swipes and short and long taps.
Conclusion:
Overall, this redesign encompasses the main features of Instagram and adds onto them with an algorithm curator and new micro-interactions that help the user reach their intended point of interest. The users will stay engaged and gain more from their in-app experience, which will in turn, help the company to gain more profit. It will also aid local businesses, since the type of customers who like their products would now be able to find their posts easily through the algorithm curator.
